Somewhat ironically, most of what I've gained through studying mathematics is less well-defined, and fits rather poorly into a table. This includes:

  • how to construct an argument clearly and logically with well-defined statements
  • patience with problems and the importance of tackling problems from different angles
  • confidence in navigating heavy mathematical notation and formulas
  • an appreciation for patterns, connections, and underlying structures in ideas

It would also be disingenuous to include some of the less tangible things I've learned through the team projects I've been a part of during my time at Olin:

  • how to communicate within a team: conveying ideas, listening to others, arranging meetings, resolving conflicts
  • the importance of maintaining a healthy team atmosphere, morale, and team work ethic
  • the importance of working with your stakeholders and balancing values and priorities

Some of these skills are on display in my portfolio.

My previous work experience includes an internship as a Mechanical Design Engineer at the Aberdeen Test Center in Maryland, and work as a Program Instructor at the DaVinci Challenge Camp hosted by the Southern University of Science and Technology in Shenzhen, China.

In my free time, I enjoy reading, drawing, going on walks with my dog, or otherwise learning something new.
